Budmen NIH Face Shield Instructions

This face shield has a 3D printed component and a laser cut acrylic component. It can help protect medical professionals, first responders and other people who may be in risky, high contact situations. It can also help free up more specialized protective gear for people working in ICUs, surgeries and with COVID-19 infected patients.

3D printing is currently the bottleneck in our ability to deliver these shields. We need your help printing more components for the shields.

Download STL from NIH Budmen Face Shield

Printing Parameters

  • PETG or PET

  • 30% infill

  • Do not rescale, print at 100%

Safety Considerations

  1. If you feel sick please take care of yourself and wait to print until you're recovered
  2. Always wash hands with soap and water for 20 seconds before touching the prints or filament - How to wash with soap and water
  3. Always wear a face mask or shield of some sort in order to protect yourself from touching your face and to prevent droplets from you speaking, sneezing, coughing, etc, entering the environment
  4. When removing a completed print from the printer, please follow these guidelines:
    1. Put on a fresh pair of gloves
    2. Pour some of hand sanitizer on your gloved hands and scrub, or wash with soap and water for 20 seconds with the gloves on
    3. Take 3D prints off each bed and place in a collection container or bag
    4. Restart all prints (with the gloves on, AFTER you have moved all the prints)
    5. Dispose of gloves
  5. It's important that other occupants of the space where your printers are also follow these rules and do not touch or breath on the printers or printed components

Can I print them from PLA / other materials?

PETG is preferable due to its mechanical properties, it doesn’t break/shatter as easily as PLA and it has better temperature resistance. Since we need the headband to be slightly elastic, we would suggest sticking to PETG. Many sterilization procedures also consist of heating the shield to over 60°C (140+ degrees Fahrenheit) - PLA isn’t suitable for such temperatures. ASA and ABS are better alternatives than PLA if PETG isn't available.

If you print with a material other than PETG please include a note with your prints letting us know what material you used. Thank you!
